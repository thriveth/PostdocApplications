### Description Applicants are invited for two postdoctoral scholar positions

  beginning Fall 2017. The postdoctoral scholars will work with Prof.
Asantha Cooray to analyze and interpret data from several infrared
imaging and spectroscopy surveys to understand the nature of dusty
starburst galaxies, faint galaxies present during reionization,
gravitationally lensed sub-mm galaxies, clustering of IR galaxies, and
the cosmic far and near-IR backgrounds.

  One of the positions is available for a postdoctoral scholar to work on
the data analysis and science studies involving the infrared background
with proposed SPHEREx (NASA) and on-going CIBER sounding rocket data to
understand the fluctuations in the near-IR background. The fellow can
also join an existing effort to follow-up interesting targets from
Herschel extragalactic surveys. This position requires some combination
of prior experience in analyzing datasets from HST/Spitzer/Herschel or
other ground and space-based observatories.

  The second position is more theoretical on topics related to cosmology,
cosmic microwave background, large-scale structure, intensity mapping and
cross-correlations among others. Experiences with CosmoMC, CAMB, halo
modeling, weak lensing and galaxy clustering calculations (related to
Euclid, SPHEREx etc) are desired. The postdoctoral fellow will provide
theory support to the team for participation in a number of upcoming wide
area surveys.

  An experience with leading or initiating an independent research
program and proposal writing is useful to work effectively in the UCI
group led by Prof. Cooray. Recent postdocs and students in the group have
made use of access to Keck Observatories through UC time and access to
Herschel, Spitzer and Hubble data through various collaborations the team
is participating.

  The initial appointment will be for two years, with a third year
contingent on successful performance and availability of funds. Salary
will be determined based on experience. The position includes a separate
research and travel fund to be used by the postdoctoral scholar.

  Applicants must have a Ph.D. or equivalent prior to beginning the
appointment.

  Applicants should send a CV, publication list, statement of research
interests, and arrange for three letters of recommendation to be
submitted via UC Irvine’s online application system, RECRUIT, located at
<https://recruit.ap.uci.edu/apply/JPF03705>.
